# Inline Micro-Disclaimers

These are short, contextual disclaimers to place directly in the UI next to specific outputs. Each is 1-2 sentences, written at or below an 8th-grade reading level, in active voice.

---

## Placement Guide

### 1. Net Worth Projection Chart

**Location:** Below or as a caption to the net worth comparison chart.

> These projections assume steady annual appreciation rates and consistent investment returns. Actual market conditions, home values, and investment performance will vary — sometimes significantly.

### 2. Tax Impact Estimates (IRA Withdrawal)

**Location:** Next to the IRA withdrawal tax and penalty breakdown.

> This tax estimate uses 2024 federal rates and stacks the withdrawal on top of your stated income. Your actual tax may differ based on deductions, credits, and state rules. A CPA can give you an exact figure.

### 3. Tax Impact Estimates (General)

**Location:** Near any tax calculation output (effective rate, annual tax liability).

> Tax estimates are approximate. They use simplified federal models and do not include all deductions, credits, or state-specific rules. Consult a CPA for your actual tax liability.

### 4. Rental Cash Flow Projections

**Location:** Below the monthly rental cash flow breakdown.

> Rental cash flow assumes consistent occupancy and the rent you entered. Actual income depends on your local market, tenant reliability, and maintenance costs — which can be large and unpredictable.

### 5. DTI Ratio and Lending Qualification

**Location:** Next to the DTI percentage display.

> This DTI estimate uses standard formulas. It is not a mortgage pre-approval. Lenders use their own criteria and may calculate your ratio differently.

### 6. Monthly Budget / Affordability Section

**Location:** Below the monthly disposable income comparison.

> Monthly cash flow estimates are projections, not guarantees. Actual expenses vary month to month. The "bad month" scenario shows what happens with zero rental income — a real possibility in any given month.

### 7. Depreciation and Recapture Estimates

**Location:** Next to depreciation or recapture tax figures.

> Depreciation is calculated on the estimated structure value (excluding land). When you sell a rental property, all claimed depreciation is taxed at 25% — this is separate from capital gains tax. Your actual depreciation basis should be confirmed by a CPA.

### 8. Commute Cost/Time Savings

**Location:** Below commute savings calculations.

> Commute savings use the IRS standard mileage rate ($0.725/mile for 2025). Your actual vehicle costs may be higher or lower. Time savings are valued at your estimated hourly rate for illustration purposes — they are not actual income.

### 9. Stress Test / Risk Scenarios

**Location:** Header or footer of the stress test section.

> Stress tests model specific scenarios (vacancy, major repair, income loss, market decline). They do not cover every possible risk. Real-world problems can occur simultaneously and may cost more than modeled.

### 10. Capital Requirements Summary

**Location:** Below the upfront capital needs breakdown.

> Closing costs, down payments, and reserve estimates are approximations. Actual costs vary by lender, location, and transaction. Get quotes from lenders and title companies for exact figures.

### 11. IRA Trajectory Comparison Chart

**Location:** Below the IRA growth comparison chart.

> This chart assumes a steady annual return on retirement investments. Actual returns vary year to year and can be negative. The gap shown grows over time due to compound growth — small differences today become large differences at retirement.

### 12. Executive Summary / Verdict

**Location:** Directly below or adjacent to the summary verdict.

> This summary reflects the assumptions and inputs you provided. It is not a recommendation. Review the full analysis and consult a financial professional before making your decision.

### 13. Home Value Appreciation Figures

**Location:** Near any displayed future home value.

> Future home values assume steady annual appreciation. Real estate markets are cyclical and local. Your home may appreciate faster, slower, or decline in value.

### 14. PMI Estimates

**Location:** Next to PMI payment or PMI drop-off information.

> PMI rates and cancellation timing are estimates. Your actual PMI rate depends on your credit score and lender. PMI is automatically cancelled when your loan balance reaches 78% of the original appraised value.
